About Me

My interest in software development started very early while playing games on the Atari and when my Dad brought home an Apple IIGS in the '80s. Later, I took my first computer course in C++ when I was a teenager and fell in love with coding. In college I took a Visual Basic course and course in C while attending the University of Illinois. In 2003 I started teaching myself to develop websites using HTML and CSS. While studying abroad in Brazil, I created a website to share my experiences with friends and family back home. A couple years ago, I started a part time Master's Program in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University. I completed courses in Java programming, Algorithms & Data Structures, and Computer Organization (i.e. how data is organized; how software talks to hardware and some coding in lower level assembly language). I recently completed an intensive coding bootcamp at Coding Dojo in San Jose. I learned 2 full stacks in Python and Javascript (MEAN) and completed my first webapps. For the future, I'll continue developing my skills and exploring my options. I am happiest when I'm creating and coding is my outlet.
